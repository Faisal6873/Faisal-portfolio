Profile

I began my career as an Electrical Site Engineer, where That technical foundation continues to influence my approach to occupational health and safety, allowing me to evaluate hazards with an engineering mindset rather than relying solely on checklist compliance. I believe effective safety management starts with understanding how work is performed, how systems interact, and where failures can occur before they lead to incidents. Throughout my career, I have applied this perspective across major infrastructure and power projects, working closely with multidisciplinary teams, contractors, and project management to identify risks, strengthen operational controls, and improve safety performance. My expertise includes incident investigation, root cause analysis, contractor HSE management, risk assessment, regulatory compliance, and the development of practical safety procedures that support both operational excellence and workforce protection.

08 — Leadership

Leading without direct authority

On multi-contractor sites, safety leadership rarely comes with a reporting line. I earn compliance by running clear toolbox talks and inductions, mentoring junior HSE staff, and giving supervisors and subcontractors a safety case they can act on — not just a rule to follow.

I sit close to project engineering, translating electrical and construction risk into language site teams actually use, and represent HSE directly in client and PMC coordination meetings.
